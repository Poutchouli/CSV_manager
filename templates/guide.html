Introduction

Welcome to the CSV Power Tool! This guide will walk you through all the features available to help you manage, edit, and analyze your CSV files effectively.

1. Uploading Files

Start by dragging and dropping one or two CSV files onto the upload area on the main page, or click "Browse Files" to select them from your computer. Once uploaded, click "Process Files" to open the editor.

2. Editing and Managing Data

Editing a Cell

Simply double-click on any cell to start editing. The cell's background will turn yellow to indicate it has been changed. Press Enter or click outside the cell to finish editing.

Adding and Deleting Rows

Click the button to add a new, empty row to the bottom of the table. To delete a row, click the icon in the "Actions" column. The row will be grayed out, and you can undo this by clicking the icon that appears.

Adding and Deleting Columns

Use the button to add a new column. To delete a column, click the icon next to the column's header. These changes are queued and will only be applied after you click "Save".

Saving Changes

After making any changes, the button will become active. Click it to apply all your queued changes (edits, deletions, additions) permanently. The page will then reload with the updated data.

3. Advanced Features

Grouping Data

Click the button and select a column. The table will collapse into groups based on the unique values in that column. You can expand/collapse each group by clicking on its header. You can also delete an entire group using the icon on the group header. Click to return to the normal view.

Merging Tables

If you have two tables, click the button. This allows you to combine tables based on a common column (a "key"). A safeguard will warn you if the merge is likely to create a very large number of rows. Here are the types of joins you can perform:

Comparing Tables

The feature is a specialized tool to find differences. It performs an Outer Join behind the scenes but only shows you the rows that are unique to each file (rows where the other table had no matching key). Using the example above, the result would show Charlie (only in Users) and the Keyboard sale (only in Sales).

Visualizing Data

Click the button and choose a column to generate an instant bar chart or histogram, helping you quickly understand the distribution of your data.
